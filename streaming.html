Notes on legality

Streaming is legally kind of a gray area, but you're fine. According to the Protecting Lawful Streaming Act of 2020:

the bill is tailored to specifically target the websites themselves, and not those who may use the sites nor those individuals who access pirated streams or unwittingly stream unauthorized copies of copyrighted works.

You're only going to (potentially) get in trouble if you are downloading or hosting the pirated media.

Setting yourself up

Getting malware from websites is so passé, and a little sad at this point. Please use an ad blocker. If you're using Chrome or Firefox, try uBlock Origin. If you're using Safari, don't. Make sure the extension is enabled (including in an incognito window) before continuing.

Tips & Tricks

  • If a website forces you to disable your ad blocker, move on. This is different than them asking you nicely to turn it off.
  • Ad blockers are not always 100% effective! Never open anything you didn’t download knowingly. Also, sometimes popup ads will show up under your window, so make sure you close out of those.
  • Never follow a site's directive to create an account, download their VPN, or install an extension. Move on.
  • Most media—unless it's very new or very obscure—will have an HD source somewhere, so keep looking if you're not satisfied.
  • Sometimes, even if you have good WiFi, certain video players just won’t load/buffer fast enough ¯\_(ツ)_/¯. You can try choosing a different player source from the same website, which is usually accessible via a dropdown menu or some buttons above/below the video player.
